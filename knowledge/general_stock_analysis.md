# General Stock Analysis Guidelines

## Fundamental Analysis
Fundamental analysis involves evaluating a company's intrinsic value by examining related economic and financial factors.

### Key Metrics
1. **Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio**: Stock price divided by earnings per share
2. **Earnings Per Share (EPS)**: Company's profit divided by outstanding shares
3. **Revenue Growth**: Year-over-year revenue increase
4. **Profit Margins**: Net income as percentage of revenue
5. **Return on Equity (ROE)**: Net income divided by shareholder equity
6. **Debt-to-Equity Ratio**: Total debt divided by shareholder equity

## Technical Analysis
Technical analysis uses historical price and volume data to predict future movements.

### Common Indicators
- **Moving Averages**: 50-day and 200-day moving averages
- **Relative Strength Index (RSI)**: Momentum indicator
- **MACD**: Trend-following momentum indicator
- **Support and Resistance Levels**: Key price levels
- **Volume**: Trading activity indicator

## Market Analysis
Understanding broader market conditions is crucial for stock analysis.

### Factors to Consider
- **Economic Indicators**: GDP growth, unemployment, inflation
- **Interest Rates**: Federal Reserve policy and rates
- **Market Sentiment**: Bull or bear market conditions
- **Sector Performance**: Industry-specific trends
- **Global Events**: Geopolitical and economic events

## Risk Assessment
Every investment carries risk that should be evaluated.

### Types of Risk
1. **Market Risk**: Overall market movements
2. **Company-Specific Risk**: Individual company issues
3. **Sector Risk**: Industry-wide challenges
4. **Economic Risk**: Macroeconomic factors
5. **Regulatory Risk**: Changes in laws and regulations

## Investment Strategies
Different approaches to stock investing based on goals and risk tolerance.

### Common Strategies
- **Value Investing**: Buying undervalued stocks
- **Growth Investing**: Investing in high-growth companies
- **Dividend Investing**: Focus on dividend-paying stocks
- **Index Investing**: Tracking market indices
- **Dollar-Cost Averaging**: Regular fixed-amount investments

## Due Diligence Checklist
- Review financial statements (income, balance sheet, cash flow)
- Analyze management team and corporate governance
- Evaluate competitive positioning
- Assess industry trends and outlook
- Review analyst reports and ratings
- Consider valuation metrics
- Understand business model and revenue streams
- Evaluate risks and potential catalysts

## Important Disclaimers
- Past performance does not guarantee future results
- All investments carry risk of loss
- Diversification is important for risk management
- Consider consulting with financial advisors
- Do your own research before investing
